How do I actually quit gaming?

Frankly speaking, it's complicated. Video game addiction is something that is quite new as it is a bit different from the traditional addictions.

How did you do it?

Quitting gaming isn't something that you can do overnight. It can sometimes feel like a daily battle, never-ending and very painful. However, it doesn't have to be that way. It is clear that quitting gaming isn't something that is easy, but that does not mean you have to use a lot of willpower to accomplish this goal. Rather, what I believe is more important is getting rid of potential cues and adding as much friction as possible to reduce the temptation to game. Despite this, only using frition isn't a good approach either. I believe it is really a mix between discipline and having systems in place in unison is what really makes the difference.

Strategy #1 - The 90-Day Detox

This strategy is essentially a variation of the dopamine detox but specifically for video games. Originally meant as a way to deal with pornography addiction, it seems to fare pretty well with all sorts of behavioural related addictions. One thing we must understand is that video game addiction is fundamentally different from various other types of addictions. Unlike something like substance abuse, there is no physical substance at play here. Rather, it is the neurotransmitter in our brain known as dopamine that is released every time we do a pleasurable activity. Dopamine, in Layman's terms, is a chemical in our brain that is released when we do sometzhing pleasurable. It is often seen is the pleasure and motivation chemical, and this chemical is very powerful and essentially drives all forms of of what we do. In fact, a lack of dopamine actually leads to Parkinson's disease. This is because dopamine is also responsible for motor function.

Strategy #2 - Competing Interests

This is a pretty interesting strategy proposed by HealthyGamerGG. Although not flawless, it does emphasize the importance of fulfilling human needs as the core of video game addiction. The one aspect that I think Healthy Gamer misses out on is the importance of resetting dopamine levels to baseline. I believe he does mention it sometimes, but it is not as emphasized as it should be. In simple terms, it is very hard to find a competing interest if you are addicted to games. This is because gaming is more stimulating compared to other activities due to the nature of video games itself and the emphasis on adding addictive elements in modern games these days. In my opinion, competing interests is simply a tool in your tool kit that you need to use with other tools to really curb your video game addiction. On its own, it simply won't be enough to quit gaming at all. I think there is definitely a bit of an overemphasis from HealthyGamerGG, as he does not seem to mention anything about a 90 day detox anywhere, which is quite an important technique for video game addiction. I feel like competing interests is one of those things that sound good in theory, but would not really work in the real world. Maybe it's just me, but with the high dopamine threshold that gaming provides, how could anything compete with it? The strategy itself could definitely be quite effective if you aren't super addicted to gaming, like if you still have other hobbies and interests but for someone like me, it wasn't really possible.

Strategy #3 - 90 Day Detox + Competing Interests

Ultimately, this is the best approach for quitting gaming. It's essentially a combination of both the techniques in an approach that has worked for me. Although one thing that is important to mention is the order in which you do this, as it makes more sense to do the 90 day detox first before you attempt to find competing interests. This is because your dopamine levels need to reset back to baseline so that when you are trying to find the competing interests, you are able to extract the full value of them instead of being constantly stimulated. There aren't really many flaws with this approach as it takes the best of both worlds and combines them in a way that allows you to quit gaming sustainably. It's definitely not easy to do, but it is the method that I personally would recommend to everyone who is trying to quit gaming. One important thing to note is that it will be pretty hard to find a competing interest. Gaming fulfills multiple needs, which is something that pretty much no other hobbies do. So in order to find a competing interest, you might have to broaden your horizons quite a bit. I would recommend finding multiple competing interests rather than a single one because you need different kinds of hobbies for different situations. You could also strive towards something really big, like a career goal and such, but it's still important to have hobbies that can fulfill the needs that gaming once did.
